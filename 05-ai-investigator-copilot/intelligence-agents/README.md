# Threat Intelligence Agent

Transforming External Intelligence into Financial Crime Detection, Investigation and AI Decision Support

---

## Executive Summary

Traditional financial crime systems are primarily reactive.

Detection models identify suspicious activity after it has occurred, investigators review alerts, and intelligence is generated during the investigative process.

However, financial crime threats evolve continuously. New sanctions evasion techniques emerge, criminal networks adapt, trade-based money laundering methodologies evolve, and geopolitical developments create entirely new risk landscapes.

The Threat Intelligence Agent introduces a fundamentally different operating model.

Rather than waiting for suspicious activity to be detected, the platform continuously consumes external intelligence, identifies emerging threats, evaluates potential impacts to existing typologies and analytics, and enriches investigative workflows with current threat context.

The Threat Intelligence Agent becomes the bridge between the external world and the Financial Crime Intelligence Platform.

# The Role of the Threat Intelligence Agent

The Threat Intelligence Agent is responsible for:

### Observing

Monitoring external intelligence sources.

### Interpreting

Identifying relevant financial crime implications.

### Assessing

Evaluating impacts to existing typologies and detection models.

### Recommending

Generating enhancement proposals for investigators, typologies and analytics.

The agent does not perform investigations.

The agent improves investigations.
